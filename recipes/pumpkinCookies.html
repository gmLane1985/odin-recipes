Pumpkin Cookies

Return to homepage

Description

A great dessert for fall, or simply any time. A pumpkin cookie made from a fresh pumpkin. Also great when you add chocolate chips!

Prep Time 20 mins
Cook Time 15 mins
Total Time 45 mins
Servings 32 cookies

Ingredients
  • 1 and 1/2 cups (340g) fresh pumpkin puree
  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour (spooned & leveled)
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 3/4 cup (12 Tbsp; 170g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 cup (200g) packed dark brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons pure maple syrup, milk, or orange juice
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract

Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. Set aside.
  2. Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and spices together in a large bowl. Set aside.
  3. Using a hand mixer or a stand mixer fitted with paddle attachment, cream the softened butter and both sugars together on medium speed until smooth, about 2 minutes. Add the egg and mix on high until combined, about 1 minute. Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed. Add the maple syrup, vanilla, and pumpkin and mix on high until combined. Mixture will look a little curdled—that’s ok
  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, then mix on low speed until combined. Dough is thick and sticky. Scoop or roll cookie dough, around 1.5 Tablespoons of dough per cookie, and place 3 inches apart on the baking sheets.
  5. Bake for 14-15 minutes or until edges appear set. The centers will look soft. Remove from the oven and allow cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. The longer the cookies cool, the better their flavor

Cover leftover iced cookies tightly and store at room temperature for up to 1 week.
