No Bake Cheesecake

Return to homepage

Description

Prep Time 35 mins
Chill Time 4 hours
Total Time 4 hours 35 mins
Servings 12 slices

Ingredients

    Crust

  • 1 ½ cups (170 g) graham cracker crumbs
  • 7 Tablespoons (100 g) butter melted

    • Filling

  • 16 oz (450 g) cream cheese softened to room temperature
  • 1 cup (150 g) powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • ⅓ cup (75 g) sour cream
  • 1 ½ cup (355 ml) heavy cream

    • Whipped Cream

  • ¾ cup (177 ml) heavy cream
  • ¼ cup (40 g) powdered sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Crust

  1. Pulverize graham crackers in food processor until finely ground into crumbs
  2. In a medium bowl mix graham cracker crumbs and melted butter until crumbs are all moistened
  3. Pour mixture into pie plate or springform pan. Use the bottom of a measuring cup to firmly pack crumbs into the bottom of the pan and (if using a pie pan) gently press up the sides. Use your fingers to pack crumbs tightly into the sides of the pie pan
  4. Refrigerate or freeze for 10-20 minutes

Filling

  1. Place cream cheese in a large bowl and, using an electric mixer, beat until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add powdered sugar and stir until combined.
  3. Add sour cream and stir well.
  4. Pour heavy cream into a separate, medium-sized bowl (preferably a chilled metal bowl). Whisk on high speed until stiff peaks are achieved (mixture will be thick, fluffy, voluminous and will hold a peak).
  5. Using a spatula, gently fold the whipped cream into cream cheese mixture until smooth and well-combined and mixture is uniform.
  6. Spread cheesecake mixture evenly into prepared graham cracker crust (be sure to fill the crust completely, if there is any gapping between the filling and the crust it will crumble when cut into).
  7. Cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours or overnight. Top with whipped cream topping before serving

Whipped Cream

  1. Combine heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract in a medium-sized mixing bowl. Use an electric mixer to beat until thick, fluffy, and stiff peaks form. Spread or pipe over cheesecake before serving (after the cheesecake has had time to set)
